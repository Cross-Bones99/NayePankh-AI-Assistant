# Volunteer Handbook

## Purpose

This handbook provides guidance to volunteers participating in NayePankh Foundation initiatives.

## Volunteer Responsibilities

Volunteers are expected to:

* Attend assigned events on time.
* Follow NGO guidelines.
* Respect beneficiaries.
* Maintain professional conduct.
* Submit reports after events.

## Event Preparation

Before attending any event:

1. Understand the event objective.
2. Review logistics and schedules.
3. Carry necessary identification.
4. Wear appropriate clothing.
5. Maintain communication with coordinators.

## Beneficiary Interaction Guidelines

Volunteers should:

* Treat all individuals with dignity.
* Listen carefully to concerns.
* Avoid judgmental behavior.
* Respect privacy.
* Provide accurate information.

## Safety Guidelines

### During Food Distribution

* Maintain hygiene standards.
* Use gloves when required.
* Follow crowd management protocols.
* Ensure safe storage of food items.

### During Awareness Programs

* Use respectful language.
* Encourage participation.
* Address misconceptions carefully.

## Reporting

After every event, volunteers should submit:

* Event location
* Number of beneficiaries
* Number of volunteers
* Resources distributed
* Challenges encountered
* Suggestions for improvement

## Communication Standards

Volunteers represent the organization and should maintain:

* Professionalism
* Honesty
* Respectful communication
* Positive attitude

## Conflict Resolution

In case of disputes:

1. Stay calm.
2. Escalate issues to coordinators.
3. Avoid public arguments.
4. Focus on problem solving.

## Code of Conduct

Volunteers must not:

* Discriminate against beneficiaries.
* Misuse organizational resources.
* Share confidential information.
* Engage in inappropriate behavior.

## Leadership Opportunities

Dedicated volunteers may progress into leadership roles such as:

* Event Coordinator
* Volunteer Manager
* Awareness Campaign Lead
* Education Program Coordinator
