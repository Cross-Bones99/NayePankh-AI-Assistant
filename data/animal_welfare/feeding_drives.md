# Stray Animal Feeding Drive Guide

## Introduction

Animal welfare initiatives help ensure that stray animals receive food, care, and protection.

## Objectives

* Reduce hunger among stray animals.
* Promote compassion.
* Raise awareness about animal welfare.
* Encourage community participation.

## Planning a Feeding Drive

### Location Selection

Identify:

* Areas with significant stray populations.
* Safe feeding zones.
* Accessible locations.

### Resource Planning

Prepare:

* Food supplies
* Water containers
* Transportation arrangements

## Feeding Guidelines

* Use suitable food items.
* Avoid harmful substances.
* Maintain cleanliness.
* Follow local regulations.

## Volunteer Responsibilities

* Prepare food.
* Distribute food safely.
* Monitor animal behavior.
* Maintain hygiene.

## Safety Precautions

* Approach animals calmly.
* Avoid sudden movements.
* Maintain safe distances.
* Follow coordinator instructions.

## Awareness Activities

Volunteers can educate communities about:

* Responsible feeding
* Animal care
* Adoption opportunities
* Compassion toward animals

## Impact Measurement

Track:

* Animals fed
* Locations covered
* Volunteer participation
* Community engagement

## Reporting

Include:

* Date
* Location
* Number of animals fed
* Resources used
* Observations
